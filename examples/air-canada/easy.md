Ticket Types & Cancellation Rules
 * Economy Basic: 0% refund. No credit for future travel. Total loss if unused.
 * Other Non-Refundable Tickets:
   * Must cancel before departure.
   * Value converts to travel credit (use within 1 year) or AC Wallet funds.
 * Ticket Validity: If you need to use a ticket value after it expires (usually 1 year), you pay a fee ($50â€“$100).

Who Gets The Money
 * Standard: Refund goes to the original purchaser and original credit card.
 * Agency / Expedia / Other Airline Bookings: Do not ask Air Canada. You must process the refund through the agency or airline you bought it from.
 * Group Bookings: Credits can be split individually or consolidated into one person's AC Wallet (if everyone agrees).

Involuntary Refunds (AC's Fault / Delays / Cancellations)
 * Definition: Flight cancelled, delayed, you were denied boarding, or bumped to a lower class.
 * If you haven't flown: Full refund of unused portion.
 * If trip is now pointless: Full refund + AC flies you back to origin.
 * Downgrade: Refund of the price difference between seats.
 * Ancillary Fees: Refunded if you didn't get the service (e.g., paid for a seat but didn't get it).

Voluntary Refunds (You Changed Your Mind)
 * Within 24 Hours of Purchase: Full refund. No penalty.
 * After 24 Hours:
   * Unused Ticket: Refund = (Price Paid) minus (Cancellation Penalty).
   * Partially Used: Refund = (Price Paid) minus (Value of flight flown) minus (Cancellation Penalty).

Special Exceptions
 * Jury Duty / Military: Full refund. Must provide jury summons, subpoena, or military orders.
 * Death:
   * Applies to: Passenger, immediate family, or travel companion.
   * Conditions: Death occurs after purchase, or the trip was specifically to visit the deceased.
   * Payout: Full refund (no fees/penalties).
   * Requirement: Must submit death certificate or funeral statement within 90 days.

Lost Paper Tickets
 * If you lose a physical paper ticket, the refund fee is $100.
